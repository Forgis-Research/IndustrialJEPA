1 Motivation

1.1 Why RUL%?

We define RUL as a fraction of remaining life:

\[\text{RUL}(t) = 1 - \frac{t}{T_{\text{failure}}}\]

This normalizes across bearings with very different absolute lifetimes (0.5h to 2.6h in XJTU-SY). A bearing at 50% of its life gets RUL=0.5 regardless of whether it lives 30 minutes or 3 hours.

1.2 Why does elapsed-time alone fail?

A naive predictor using only elapsed time cannot work across machines because:

  1. Unknown total lifetime at test time — we don’t know when failure will occur
  2. High variability — XJTU-SY lifetimes range 5x (0.5h to 2.6h)
  3. Cross-dataset transfer — FEMTO bearings run at different loads/speeds than XJTU-SY

The elapsed-time baseline achieves RMSE = 0.224 in-domain and 0.367 cross-domain. Signal-based methods must beat these to be useful.

#| label: fig-waveforms
#| fig-cap: "Representative vibration waveforms from a FEMTO bearing at different degradation stages. RMS amplitude increases as the bearing approaches failure."

ep_id = list(femto_eps.keys())[0]
snaps = femto_eps[ep_id]
n = len(snaps)

early_idx = n // 10
mid_idx = n // 2
late_idx = int(n * 0.9)

fig, axes = plt.subplots(3, 1, figsize=(12, 6), sharex=True)
labels = [('Early (RUL=0.9)', early_idx, 'steelblue'),
          ('Mid (RUL=0.5)', mid_idx, 'orange'),
          ('Late (RUL=0.1)', late_idx, 'red')]

for ax, (label, idx, color) in zip(axes, labels):
    sig = snaps[idx]['signal'][:1024]
    t = np.arange(len(sig)) / 25600
    ax.plot(t * 1000, sig, color=color, linewidth=0.5, alpha=0.8)
    ax.set_ylabel('Amplitude')
    ax.set_title(label)
    rms = np.sqrt(np.mean(sig**2))
    ax.text(0.02, 0.95, f'RMS={rms:.4f}', transform=ax.transAxes, va='top', fontsize=10, color=color)

axes[-1].set_xlabel('Time (ms)')
plt.suptitle(f'Bearing Waveforms: {ep_id}', y=1.01)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

method_order = [
    'constant_mean', 'elapsed_time_linear',
    'envelope_rms_lstm', 'random_jepa_lstm',
    'cnn_lstm_e2e', 'cnn_gru_mha',
    'handcrafted_lstm', 'jepa_lstm',
    'handcrafted_mlp', 'transformer_handcrafted',
]

for method in method_order:
    r = results.get(method, {})
    if 'rmse_mean' in r:
        rmse = r['rmse_mean']
        std = r['rmse_std']
        vs = (time_rmse - rmse) / time_rmse * 100
        print(f'{method:<32} {rmse:8.4f} {std:7.4f} {vs:+13.1f}%')
    elif 'rmse' in r:
        rmse = r['rmse']
        vs = (time_rmse - rmse) / time_rmse * 100
        print(f'{method:<32} {rmse:8.4f} {"":>7} {vs:+13.1f}%')

Piecewise labels are harder because the constant-RUL healthy phase makes prediction ambiguous — the signal changes but the label stays at 1.0.

9 Encoder Analysis

#| label: fig-encoder
#| fig-cap: "What each encoder learns. JEPA captures waveform texture; temporal contrastive captures spectral centroid shift (the dominant degradation indicator)."

enc_img = plt.imread(str(PLOTS_DIR / 'v8_encoder_analysis.png'))

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Mechanistic explanation: JEPA’s objective (predict masked patches from context) learns waveform texture and periodicity. Temporal contrastive’s objective (early snapshots negative to late) forces the encoder to learn what changes over bearing life — primarily the spectral centroid shift as surface roughness increases.

10 Summary and Honest Limitations

10.1 Key Results

10.2 Honest Limitations

  1. JEPA does not capture spectral centroid shift (primary RUL indicator). Max per-dim correlation: 0.144 vs 0.585 for spectral centroid.

  2. JEPA pretraining is unstable on heterogeneous multi-source data. Loss oscillates after epoch 2; best checkpoint is always early.

  3. Only 23 episodes (16 FEMTO + 7 XJTU-SY). 5-seed results have high variance.

  4. Temporal contrastive requires run-to-failure episode structure. Self-supervised but not label-free.

  5. Cross-dataset comparison is limited to two datasets with different operating conditions.

10.3 Novel Contribution

Temporal contrastive pretraining using run-to-failure episode structure learns spectral centroid-aligned representations that transfer cross-dataset. This is the first demonstration of JEPA vs contrastive for RUL% prediction with a mechanistic explanation of why contrastive transfers better. The hybrid JEPA+HC result (0.055 RMSE, +75.5% vs time-only) shows JEPA captures waveform features orthogonal to handcrafted spectral statistics.
