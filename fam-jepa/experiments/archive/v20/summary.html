TL;DR

  • Predictor finetuning (pred-FT) vs scratch at 5% labels is the statistically supported contribution: paired t(4) = 3.03, p = 0.039, Cohen’s d = 1.35 on C-MAPSS FD001 per-window F1 (5 seeds). F1w = 0.261 (pred-FT) vs 0.035 (scratch).
  • Crossover characterized at N=10 on FD001: pred-FT wins at ≤10% labels, E2E wins at ≥20%.
    • 5% (Phase 7): pred-FT 0.229 ± 0.141 vs E2E 0.124 ± 0.201. Wilcoxon p=0.065 (miss). Collapses pred 1/10, e2e 7/10.
    • 10% (Phase 8): pred-FT 0.291 ± 0.120 vs E2E 0.133 ± 0.164. Paired t p=0.023, Wilcoxon p=0.010, d=0.87. Collapses pred 0/10, e2e 4/10.
    • 20% (Phase 9): pred-FT 0.276 ± 0.184 vs E2E 0.312 ± 0.102. p=0.57 (tied). Collapses pred 2/10, e2e 0/10 (flipped!).
    • 50-100% (Phase 0+5, N=5): pred-FT and E2E statistically tied.
  • FD003 replication (Phase 10, N=5, 200-ep pretrain): pred-FT significantly beats E2E at 100% labels on FD003 (pred 0.270 vs e2e 0.123, paired t p=0.043, d=1.25). Direction differs from FD001 (where E2E wins at 100%) because E2E collapses 2/5 seeds on FD003 even at full labels.
  • Longer pretraining trades scarce-label adaptability for rich-label ceiling: FD003 100% pred-FT 200-ep 0.270 vs 100-ep 0.146 (+0.124). FD003 5-10% pred-FT: 200-ep WORSE by 0.05-0.09. Not seen on FD001 (only 200-ep ckpt tested).
  • FD002 replication (Phase 11, 150-ep pretrain, N=5): Multi-op-condition subset. At 100% E2E significantly beats pred-FT (p=0.038, 0.426 vs 0.315). At 10% exact tie (0.258 vs 0.259). At 5% pred-FT borderline (p=0.070, 0.289 vs 0.216). Zero seed-level collapses on FD002 across all 30 runs. FD002’s multi-condition data provides enough supervisory diversity that E2E’s collapse failure mode disappears - and when E2E does not collapse, pred-FT does not win.
  • Unified cross-subset statement (refined): pred-FT wins where E2E collapses. When E2E has enough diverse data to learn reliably, E2E’s higher capacity prevails. Pred-FT’s claim is a robustness claim, not a mean-capacity claim. Three subsets tested, significance at (FD001@10%, p=0.023), (FD003@100%, p=0.043), and (FD002@100%, p=0.038 for E2E).
  • Reviewer round 2 verdict: 6/10 weak accept (up from 4/10). Verified all headline p-values independently. Remaining concern: FD003 checkpoint confound at low labels - 200-ep gives different low-label numbers than 100-ep. Addressed in Tab 3 by reporting both rows and acknowledging the sensitivity.
  • SIGReg-pred pretraining beats EMA: F1w 0.451 vs 0.391 at 100% labels (+0.06), RMSE 13.71 vs 16.90 (-3.19). Lower variance at 5% (std 3.55 vs 6.83 on RMSE).
  • PSM pred-FT fails: AUROC near chance (0.46-0.55) across modes. Chronological test-split distribution shift defeats supervised fine-tuning. Mahalanobis from pretrained encoder is the PSM recipe (PA-F1 0.813).
  • Chronos-T5-tiny (8.4M) matches FAM pred-FT (2.37M) at linear-probe vs pred-FT setting: F1w 0.419 vs 0.391 (within CI). FAM E2E wins overall at 100% labels (F1w 0.408, RMSE 14.96).
  • Multi-domain: FAM + Mahalanobis outperforms MTS-JEPA on SMAP (+0.46 PA-F1), MSL (+0.37), PSM (+0.20). Uses ONE architecture across 7 datasets.
  • NeurIPS reviewer gave 4/10 borderline reject; paper revisions pushed in-session addressed: stale 1.26M/198K param refs (→ 2.37M/790K), removed unsupported “560x” claim, added paired t-tests, filled all TODO sections, framed PSM failure honestly in Limitations.

Phase-by-phase detail

Phase 0a: Build predictor-FT infrastructure (~30 min)

pred_ft_utils.py implements five downstream modes (probe_h, frozen_multi, pred_ft, e2e, scratch) with a shared scalar-RUL regression objective and per-window F1 derived by thresholding at each window boundary (W=16).

Key bug caught during bring-up: V17 predictor outputs have std ~5 (no trailing LayerNorm after the predictor’s MLP) while h_past has std ~1. Without a LayerNorm in MultiHorizonHead before the linear layer, the sigmoid saturates at init and frozen_multi can’t learn. Added nn.LayerNorm pre-head. Also verified that predictor outputs have cos_sim > 0.98 between h_hat_1 and h_hat_15 - the horizon-conditioning signal is weak in the pretrained predictor, which is WHY frozen_multi underperforms probe_h (0.148 vs 0.299 F1w) and why pred-FT’s value is in reshaping the predictor, not in extracting forward rollouts.

Phase 0b: Five-mode label sweep (8.5 min, 50 runs)

Five modes × two budgets (100%, 5%) × five seeds = 50 runs on C-MAPSS FD001. Pred-FT beats scratch at 5% with p=0.039 (clean significant finding). Pred-FT vs E2E gap (0.084 F1w in favor of pred-FT at 5%) is not significant at N=5. See headline table above.

Phase 1: Multi-domain benchmark aggregation

Built phase1_aggregate.py to collate v18/v19/v20 JSONs into a single table. FAM+Mahalanobis numbers from v18/v19: - SMAP PA-F1 = 0.793 ± 0.014 (v18 phase 4) - MSL PA-F1 = 0.707 ± 0.050 (v18 phase 4) - PSM PA-F1 = 0.813 ± 0.048 (v19 phase 1) - MBA PA-F1 = 0.551 ± 0.054 (v19 phase 2) - SMD PA-F1 = 0.252 ± 0.017 (v19 phase 4) - Paderborn macro-F1 = 0.781 ± 0.035 (v19 phase 5)

Phase 1b: PSM pred-FT (NEGATIVE RESULT)

Attempted pred-FT on PSM using v19 PSM ckpts with chronological 60/10/30 split of the labeled test timeline. Result: AUROCw in [0.46, 0.55] across all modes and seeds - essentially chance. The chronological split induces anomaly-distribution shift between FT-train and FT-eval halves that defeats supervised FT. Mahalanobis (v19) doesn’t share this problem and remains the PSM recipe.

Phase 2: Chronos-T5-tiny baseline (0.3 min, 3 seeds)

Chronos-T5-tiny (8.4M): F1w 0.419 ± 0.028, RMSE 16.80 ± 1.54 on frozen linear probe. FAM pred-FT (2.37M) matches within CI (F1w 0.391 ± 0.085, RMSE 16.90 ± 1.71). FAM E2E is the winner at 100% labels (F1w 0.408, RMSE 14.96).

Phase 3b: EMA vs SIGReg pretraining (1.5 min, 20 runs)

SIGReg-pred pretraining (applied at predictor output during v17 phase 3) beats EMA default on FD001 pred-FT: - 100%: F1w 0.451 vs 0.391 (+0.06), RMSE 13.71 vs 16.90 (-3.19) - 5%: F1w tied, but RMSE std drops from 6.83 to 3.55 (more stable)

SIGReg-enc (applied at encoder output) is between the two.

Phase 5: Full label-efficiency curve (2.1 min, 30 runs)

Extended Phase 0b with 50%, 20%, 10% budgets for pred-FT and E2E. Result table above. Crossover is at ~10%, not uniform across all low budgets.

Phase 6: FD003 replication (7.5 min)

Pretrained V17 arch on FD003 (100 epochs, 3.4 min) then ran pred-FT + E2E at 5 label budgets (50 downstream runs). FD003 numbers are absolutely lower than FD001 (F1w 0.146 vs 0.391 at 100%) likely because the FD003 pretraining was undertrained (100 epochs vs v17’s 200 epochs on FD001). But the qualitative crossover pattern replicates:

Caveats for the user

  1. Do not quote “pred-FT beats E2E” anywhere - the numbers don’t support it at N=5. Use “pred-FT is more robust (no collapses) and has a directional advantage”. The pred-FT-vs-scratch claim (p=0.039) is the safe one.
  2. The PSM pred-FT failure is in Limitations, not swept under the rug. If a reviewer asks “does pred-FT work on all your datasets?” the answer is No, it works on C-MAPSS-like supervised-RUL settings; Mahalanobis is the anomaly recipe.
  3. Phase 6 FD003 F1w numbers (at 100% labels: 0.04-0.22, mean 0.146) are considerably lower than FD001’s 0.39. FD003 pretraining was 100 epochs (vs 200 on FD001), so absolute numbers are undertrained. The crossover direction DOES match FD001 (pred-FT > e2e at 5%, e2e > pred-FT at 100%), but neither individual cell reaches p<0.05 at N=5. A 200-epoch FD003 pretraining run would likely tighten the story.
  4. The paper abstract says “2.37M-parameter” FAM - this is the context encoder + predictor. The EMA target encoder adds another 1.85M during pretraining but is dropped at inference. Total inference footprint = 2.37M.
