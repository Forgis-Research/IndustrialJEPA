V31 Analysis: Label Fraction Bug Fix and 10% Label Results

Published

April 26, 2026

Overview

V31 session: fixed the label_fraction bug in v30 for single-entity datasets (MBA, BATADAL, PSM, ETTm1, GECCO), re-ran all 11 datasets at 10% labels, and updated paper.tex with v30/v31 numbers.

Bug: For single-entity datasets (n_train_entities == 1), the v30 label_fraction code computed max(1, round(1 * 0.1)) = 1, leaving all training data unchanged. This made the 10% and 100% results identical.

Fix: For single-entity datasets, truncate the training time series to the first label_fraction * T timesteps (time truncation instead of entity subsampling).

Key finding: GECCO at 10% has zero positive labels (first anomaly at t=16265, beyond 10% truncation point t=6978). This is an honest negative result - reported as such.

Section 3: GECCO Zero-Label Anomaly

Critical finding: GECCO at 10% labels has 0 positive labels in training. The first anomaly in the GECCO training time series occurs at t=16,265. The 10% truncation cuts at t=6,978. Therefore the finetuning loss is based entirely on negative (no-event) examples. The model learns to predict 0 probability everywhere, which explains the below-chance h-AUROC.

This is an honest limitation of time-truncation label fraction for datasets where anomalies are temporally concentrated in the latter half of the operational period.
